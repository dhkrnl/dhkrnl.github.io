AgniCycle

Browser-native Combustion Research Platform

AgniCycle is a free, browser-native platform for combustion and EV research — providing research-grade tools for engine thermodynamics, fuel spray dynamics, and EV battery modelling. No installation, no license fees, no sign-in required.

Visit agnicycle.com
www.agnicycle.com — opens in a new tab
Platform tools

Engine Simulator v1.3 · Live

Zero-dimensional transient solver with 0.25° crank-angle resolution. Supports 12+ fuels — H₂, NH₃, gasoline, diesel, methanol, CNG, DME, and custom blends. Knock prediction, NOₓ estimation, and P-V diagrams included.

Fuel Spray Calculator Beta

Droplet SMD, spray penetration, cone angle, and D² evaporation modelling. Export results as CSV or SVG for publication-ready figures.

EV Battery Suite Beta

Equivalent Circuit Model simulations for 6+ chemistries — NMC-811, LFP, NCA, LCO, and more. SOC/SOH estimation, thermal coupling, CC-CV charging, and degradation analysis.

CFD Calculators Live

Four browser-based tools for Reynolds numbers, y⁺ estimation, turbulence properties, and non-dimensional numbers — instantly accessible without any setup.

CFD Training & Consulting Booking Open

1-on-1 sessions with an active combustion researcher covering CONVERGE, GT-Power, and HPC workflows — tailored for graduate students and engineers transitioning into CFD.

The motivation

Research-grade simulation is typically locked behind expensive institutional licenses (GT-Power, Ricardo WAVE, CONVERGE) or complex local setups. AgniCycle was built to close that gap — a free, browser-native platform delivering peer-validated tools for combustion and EV battery research, accessible to any researcher or student anywhere. "Agni" is Sanskrit for fire, a nod to combustion research and the IIT tradition.
